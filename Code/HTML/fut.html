Public Cloud Market Starts to Level Out

The public cloud market, while still prominent, is losing some popularity from the other cloud services. This is most likely because more and more people are starting to care about their personal information being accessed anywhere, so the private and hybrid cloud services are becoming more and more popular.

More People are Investing in the Cloud

There is a steady increase of cloud computing use. This means that cloud computing is still popular and has not reached its peak popularity yet. As more and more people use the cloud, cloud traffic will grow to an estimated value of 17.1 zetabytes (336,861,356,762 DVDs) by 2020. Click here to learn about the advantages of cloud computing.

The Multi-Cloud Strategy is Becoming more Prominent

There has been a decrease in the single cloud strategy and an increase in the multi cloud strategy. The multi cloud strategy is where two or more cloud computing services are used, so people don't have to be on the same cloud system all of the time. The multi cloud strategy has increased by 4 percent, while the Cloud system has decreased by 8 percent. With these statistics, some expect that the multi cloud system to become more prominent through 2020, and the single cloud system to do the opposite. Market Growth

The global market revenue for cloud computing was about $260 billion by the end of 2017. This number is expected to grow about 20% each year, amounting at $320 billion by the end of this year. IaaS and PaaS have an annual growth of 47%, making them the fastest growing cloud service. The reason IaaS ia growing quickly is because it is readily available, and has low maintenance. This means companies can quickly and easily rent out something on the cloud, and wasting less time maintaining it on their own. PaaS is popular due to internet-connected platforms like social media and video games. Much of our lives are online, and PaaS is growing with us. Finally, with a 31% growth rate, Microsoft and Salesforce lead the SaaS industry in cloud computing. UCaaS (Unified Communications as a Service), a cloud delivery model, has around a 20% growth rate which is close to the growth rate of the private and public cloud.

Predictions

Modular Software

Since the amount of hardware demanded by the cloud in the form of IaaS will increase, the amount of software required will follow. Modular software is where components of the software can be replaced without the program shutting down. It will be of higher demand since individual programs will only get larger and more complex. The demand of modular software will also be due to cloud services being spread apart into the servers of different cloud services.

Software Drifts from Hardware

Some people argue that software will start to ‘float’ away from hardware. This is primarily due to a growing trend where businesses are using more cloud applications to automate business. Eventually this will result in a much more abstract environment where additional software has to go through several filters before interacting with hardware. This would make applications running on the platform, given by the provider, will have never even known of the hardware.

Clouds will Become Specialized

Clouds, today, are differentiated based on whether they provide IaaS, PaaS, or SaaS. In the future, some predict that more specialized clouds will emerge to deal with new and existing tasks. This allows companies to move more specific tasks to the cloud rather than having onsite infrastructure to deal with it.

Data Centers Become Ecosystems

With the progress of hardware and software, data centers are expected to become more automated. A central system that controls the hardware with software, will grow and shrink the amount of infrastructure dedicated to a specific workload. They’re also expected to patch and update equipment regularly, hence being comparable to a biological ecosystem.

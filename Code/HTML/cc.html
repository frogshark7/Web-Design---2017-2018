Definition

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services the wirelessly through the internet, or the cloud.

Types

IaaS
PaaS
SaaS
Infrastructure as a Service is when you rent a Infrastructure like a server, storage, operating systems, exedra from a wireless provider. Some IaaS examples are Servers and storage, Networking firewalls and security, a Data center, and a physical plant or building.
Platform as a Service is a bit broader than infrastructure as a service, since infrastructure as a Service is a subsection of Platform as a Service. Platform as a Service is a Cloud computing service Based off of a computer platform, like google docs. It is used with operating systems, Development tools on a computer, Database management, Business analytics, and all of the uses listed under IaaS.
Software as a Service is a a service where something is downloaded as a software onto a device. This counts as apps, launchers, and all of the uses listed in Platform as a Service and Infrastructure as a Service.

SaaS

Software as a Service is like a bank. It protects the valuables and privacy of its customers, while providing reliable service. Software as a Service is a service where something is downloaded as a software onto a device. This counts as apps, launchers, and all of the uses listed in Platform as a Service and Infrastructure as a Service. Software as a Service is also a way of transmitting applications over the Cloud, Hence the word Software in its name. Because of this, Software as a Service Frees up time and hassle from managing Software and hardware. Some advantages of this service are its Painless and hassle free Upgrades, its low initial cost, it is integrated into the computer system seamlessly, and it is adopted by many. Software as a Service was created during the time of the earliest computers-sometime in the 1960’s. Around that time, SaaS was just called a ‘Time Sharing System’. This system involved terminals of keyboards and monitors hooked up to one mainframe CPU, where all files remained on that mainframe. This created a network of computers hooked up to one main system, with all of the information stored in one place, with complete access. The time sharing system continued to be used throughout the 1980’s, until computers got too small for it. Once that happened, in 1990, SaaS turned wireless. The same idea of the Time Sharing System was used in a Local Area Network, or LAN. This LAN service was a big flop, because it was too confusing for most businesses. After the LAN service went down, the Application Service Provider industry took over. This ended up being a failure, but it’s benefits are very similar to the modern SaaS: Low Cost, Painless upgrading, and secure. Then, in 1998, Concur became the first SaaS company, and a year later, Salesforce created its own Software as a Service network. Over the years, Software as a Service has become very popular with many businesses around the globe, and has turned into a very useful form of cloud computing.
